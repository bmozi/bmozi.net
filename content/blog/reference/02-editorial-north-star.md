# Editorial North Star — private reference (never published)

Every article or talk kit across all five series is written generically, but it is
*checked* against two things before release: the mission and the WGU framework signals
it exercises — Leadership Principles, Key Results, Cultural Beliefs, or this hub's
working architecture crosswalk. This file is the standing correlation map. If a draft
can't name its framework signals, it isn't ready.

**The mission lens.** Improve quality and expand access to opportunity; learning
independent of time or place; credentials credible to institutions and employers.
Translated for editorial use: *every piece must ultimately serve the person being
processed by the system — the reader's customer, student, patient, or applicant — and
must make excellent practice more accessible, not more gatekept.* Writing that shows off
instead of teaching fails the mission test.

## Working crosswalk → where it lives in the writing

**Aim True** — every series plan states its purpose and audience before its content;
articles that drift from the arc get cut. (The four `00-series-plan.md` files plus The
Podium's `00-talk-portfolio.md`.)

**Student Obsessed** — The Seams 01 (complaints as system outputs) and Architect's Mind 07
(Systems That Process People) are this principle at full strength: the person on the
other end of the screen is the unit of design.

**Advance Equity** — Seams for Everyone exists for this: making system literacy available
to people the jargon excludes; Architect's Mind 07 covers dignity, notice, and appeal
paths as design requirements.

**Integrity** — The Seams 03 (Nothing Changes Silently), Getting It Right 07 (tamper-evident
records) and 08 (privacy vs. permanence, resolved honestly rather than conveniently).

**Sound Judgment** — the whole of The Architect's Mind, especially 01 (decision pacing)
and 03 (hype cycles as evidence streams).

**One-by-One** — The Seams 06 and Getting It Right 10: the unit is one item, one owner,
one timer — never an aggregate that hides the individual stuck inside it. Seams for
Everyone 05 (The Tracking Number) teaches it to everyone else.

**Align & Commit** — Architect's Mind 05 (consensus or cowardice; commit to decisions as
if they were your own) and 04 (Conway's law: design teams and seams as one act).

**Earn Trust** — The Seams 05 (Prosecute Your Own Architecture) is this principle as
method; The Seams 09 makes trust claims falsifiable.

**Inspire & Develop** — the existence of the tiered portfolio itself: ELI5 for the room,
practitioner depth for the builders, judgment essays for the leaders — everyone gets a
rung. Architect's Mind 08 (Designing for Your Own Absence) is development as legacy.

**Learning** — Getting It Right's fixed spine (naive → breaks → correct → THE TEST) is
benchmark-against-the-best in article form; hype-cycle residue reading (Architect's Mind 03)
is learning from the industry's mistakes as well as our own.

**Imagine Boldly** — The Seams 10 and Architect's Mind 02: hold the ten-year picture;
let slices revise it without dissolving it.

**Create & Innovate** — The Seams 07 (Choose Your Ground): invent only where the domain
is unserved; slough off the rest. The best-of-the-past bias runs through every
"what survived the hype" passage.

**Urgency** — slices in ninety days, decisions paced by reversibility, series produced
in full so release never stalls. Speed relative to risk, everywhere.

**Deliver Results** — The Seams 09 (numbers, owners, consequences) and the closing rule
of every business argument: attribution or it didn't happen; activity is not impact.

## Standing editorial checks (apply to every future draft)

1. Names its principles — at least two, honestly, or it's filler.
2. Serves the processed person — the reader's customer is visible in the piece.
3. Generic and composite — no employer specifics, ever; disclaimers intact.
4. Teaches up and down — an expert learns a nuance, a newcomer learns a concept.
5. Falsifiable where it claims — numbers, tests, or challenges the reader can run.

# The Program on Trial — Prosecution, Verdicts, and Fixes

*The program that prescribes prosecution stood trial on its own terms. The prosecution was conducted by a fresh-eyes reviewer with no authorship attachment, briefed to be hostile; fourteen charges were returned. This document records each charge honestly, renders a verdict, and — where convicted — names the fix with its trigger. Per the charter: nothing deleted, revised in the open, and the losses recorded in the first paragraph: **the program was convicted, in whole or part, on eleven of fourteen charges.** What follows is why that is the program working, not failing — but only if the fixes actually run.*

## The three charges pressed hardest, answered first

**Charge 12 — "A slow-motion confidentiality incident with a weekly release cadence." VERDICT: CONVICTED IN SUBSTANTIAL PART — the gravest finding.**
Partial defense on one count: the /wgu hub is *not* public — it sits behind the access gate, noindexed, crawler-blocked. But the defense ends there. The convictions stand: the LinkedIn series is public and its "generic" five failure modes map 1:1 to the gated diagnosis in minutes for any colleague shown both; the release cadence would publicly narrate the tenure in near-real-time; "verbatim gems" from 1:1s must never be publication feedstock; and — the inexcusable gap — seven planning files and not one policy-review step.
**FIXES, effective immediately:** (1) Nothing publishes until WGU's social-media, outside-activity, and acceptable-use policies are read and satisfied — week-one task, before Part 1 ships. (2) A staleness buffer: no article publishes within a quarter of the real events that could be mapped to it; the series was written in advance precisely so it *can* wait. (3) Verbatim gems live in private field notes only — a hard rule, added to the field kit's conduct section in spirit and honored absolutely. (4) The gate credentials are never shared; the hub is a private notebook, not a portfolio to show colleagues — anything shown internally gets re-derived in internal tools under internal policy. (5) Employment-agreement IP language gets read before any claim of personal ownership over role-adjacent work products.

**Charges 6 + 3 — "A confirmation engine inside an echo chamber." VERDICT: CONVICTED — the deepest finding.**
The prosecution is right that every prior "prosecution" was written inside the same collaboration that wrote the designs, and that the field kit's note template pre-structures perception into the thesis's categories. The register could confirm what its author arrived believing, and he would call it evidence.
**FIXES:** (1) *Pre-registered falsification criteria, sealed with the baseline before Day 1:* the seams thesis is materially wrong if a majority of traced failure stories ran THROUGH a single system's own workflow (not around/between); the field kit's classification is invalid unless at least one traced story per week genuinely resists classification (all-clean sorting = the template is leading the witness). (2) *Invert E2's success metric:* the Days 31–60 sessions succeed only if the team materially changes **three or more** of the nine v2 decisions; fewer than two changed is treated as failed elicitation — evidence the room deferred rather than engaged — never as vindication. (3) *Opposing counsel becomes human:* no future document may bear the word "prosecution" until at least one person who is neither John nor an AI has attacked it; the Days 31–60 team is the first real opposing counsel this work will ever face, and that is to be said to them in exactly those words. (4) The next prosecution of any design is written by John *alone, unaided*, before any AI involvement — the signature move, demonstrated solo (this also answers Charge 11's atrophy count).

**Charge 2 — "The time budget is insolvent." VERDICT: CONVICTED.**
The arithmetic holds: 12–18 hours/week of program overhead in months 1–3 is a second job, and a program with no designed degradation path collapses silently — violating the charter's ninth commitment before October.
**FIXES:** (1) *A published shedding order,* effective now — under load, loops die in this sequence: publishing cadence (weekly → monthly) first; katas and crash-reports (monthly → quarterly) second; the quarterly artifact (→ semiannual) third. *Never shed:* the daily journal line (5 min), weekly prediction resolution (15 min), and the field kit during Days 1–30. (2) A hard cap: program overhead ≤5 hours/week after month one; anything that doesn't fit moves down the shedding order, out loud, in the Monday check-in. (3) Shedding is announced, not confessed — "I dropped kata night this month because Slice 1 needed the hours" is the ninth commitment kept, not broken.

## The remaining verdicts, briefly and honestly

**Charge 1 (the program is its own diagnosed failure mode — comprehensive pre-modeling): PARTIAL.** Acquitted on framing — pre-employment preparation explicitly labeled hypothesis is not the anti-canon's unused-repository pattern. Convicted on the drift: the study guide's "memorize it cold" converted hypothesis into identity. *Fixed in the study guide itself: own the questions cold; hold the answers loosely.*

**Charge 4 (confidence laundering — tiers for others' claims, none for our own): CONVICTED.** The premortem "30%" is one 1989 lab study [E-lab, single]; "servant leadership is the one style with meta-analytic support" overstated (transformational has decades — the honest claim is *incremental* validity); the five-year SIS median is [P/F, uncited range]; and the load-bearing theses now carry their own tiers: the seams thesis **[P]**, the ledger thesis **[P]**, the five failure modes **[P — one institution's public reviews plus cross-industry pattern-match]**. *The curriculum and canon inherit these corrections at next revision; this paragraph is the tier record until then.*

**Charge 5 (memorizing estimates as facts): CONVICTED.** *Fixed in the study guide: method cold, digits loose, one verified number.*

**Charge 7 (colleagues cast in scripted roles): CONVICTED.** *Fixed in the field kit: currencies and psychologies demoted to probability-weighted hypotheses; the scripted Salesforce opener removed; role-refusal named as evidence, not obstacle.*

**Charge 8 (dates before facts; ratification theater): PARTIAL.** "Cadence, not contract" was already the plan's own language — but convicted on the vindication expectation (fixed via the inverted E2 metric above) and on Slice-1 fragility: **added fallback** — if institutional process makes production shipment impossible by Day 90, Slice 1's minimum honest form is a *measured instrument*: the black-hole rate quantified on real data with a named owner and published method. A measurement nobody had is still one class of invisibility ended, and the guild precondition accepts it.

**Charge 9 (self-graded, gameable calibration): CONVICTED.** **Fixes:** PARTIAL may resolve at most 20% of statements — beyond that, the statements were written to be ungradeable and that itself is the finding; ambiguous statements get resolution criteria written *before* Day 1; one trusted human outside WGU grades the contestable resolutions at Day 90; the prediction log stays private and is never cited for influence — the "reliably right in public" ledger builds only from predictions made openly in work channels, which removes the safe-bet incentive from the private instrument.

**Charge 10 (self-terminating feedback rituals): PARTIAL.** The loops are better than nothing (the mechanism evidence stands), but convicted on the closed loop: **fixes** — the quarterly self-prosecution goes to one trusted human reader, every quarter, no exceptions; the "can stakeholders name what architecture did for them" question is answered by *asking three actual stakeholders*; the Niemi count counts only artifacts and decisions with named non-John operators.

**Charge 11 (AI as policy exposure, SPOF, atrophy engine): CONVICTED on all three counts.** **Fixes:** (1) A bright line, effective now: no employer-internal facts — no names, incidents, metrics, org dynamics, or documents — enter this or any personal AI tool; check-ins and baselines run on John's abstracted judgments only, and the week-one policy reading (Charge 12, fix 1) governs everything; if WGU policy is stricter, WGU policy wins without negotiation. (2) The manual fallback, documented here: a paper decision journal, calendar-blocked weekly review using the curriculum's own question list, and the quarterly checklist printed — the program must run on paper or it isn't a practice, it's a subscription. (3) The solo-prosecution requirement from Charge 3 stands as the atrophy test, quarterly.

**Charge 13 (the student as rhetorical tiebreaker): CONVICTED, and it should sting.** Zero students in the corpus; self-artifacts throughout; the one student-touching deliverable is the most fragile. Partial defense: a preparation phase *is* self-development, and pretending otherwise would be the deeper dishonesty. **Fixes:** front-line staff who speak with students daily are elevated in the 1:1 roster explicitly *as the students' proxy voice*, and their verbatim gems are weighted accordingly; any sanctioned channel to actual student voices (advisory panels, support-call shadowing with permission) is pursued in Q1, not deferred; and one student-touching number joins the personal scorecard from the first quarter — even if it is only the black-hole measurement — so the north star has a needle before it has a monument.

**Charge 14 (spending others' time without authority): PARTIAL.** The preconditions already gate the guild, but convicted on scale: **fix** — the guild starts with *one* ritual (the Design Clinic), begun only at the invitation or explicit sponsorship of the teams' own leadership, with its time cost stated on the invitation; the other three rituals earn their calendars the way everything else here earns expansion — by the first one being worth attending.

## What survives — the strength assessment

The prosecution, pressed to name what it could not kill, would concede this list, and it is substantial: the listen-first sequencing with real dates; the slice strategy with measured exits; the charter's checkable commitments; the shedding of certification folklore for evidence-weighted practice; the premortem/calibration/journal loop *as mechanisms* (convicted only in their self-graded implementation, now fixed); the guild's self-dissolving design; the writing's quality and its staleness-buffered value; and — demonstrated by this very document — the program's one genuinely rare property: **it can lose an argument with reality in writing, on its own site, and become more likely to succeed because of it.** The prosecution's final irony cuts both ways: yes, every prior prosecution was in-house — but the program responded to that charge by commissioning the most hostile external review it could construct and convicting itself eleven times. The habit is real. The fixes make it honest.

**Standing order:** this trial reruns at Day 90 with real evidence, a human reader, and John writing the prosecution's first draft alone.

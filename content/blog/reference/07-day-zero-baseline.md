# The Day-0 Baseline — Sealed Beliefs, To Be Graded

*The instrument is mine; the beliefs must be yours. Before Day 1, assign each statement a probability (50% = coin flip, 95% = near-certain), add any statements of your own, then SEAL it: date it, stop editing it, and grade it at Day 30 and Day 90. Hindsight bias will try to convince future-you that you "knew it all along" — this document is the only witness that can't be talked out of its testimony. Principles under load: **Integrity** (honesty with no witness required), **Sound Judgment** (calibration is judgment, measured), **Learning** (the misses are the curriculum).*

**How to fill it in (one evening):** Go with your gut probability, quickly — deliberation beyond thirty seconds per item mostly adds anchoring, not accuracy. For each, one line of "what would change my mind" — naming the evidence in advance is what makes grading honest. Add 5–10 statements of your own in the blank sections; the pre-seeded ones are candidates drawn from our work, and you should strike any that don't feel like real beliefs you hold.

**Grading:** at Day 30 and Day 90, mark each resolved statement TRUE/FALSE/PARTIAL, note your probability, and compute the miss. High-confidence misses (≥80% and wrong) get a one-paragraph post-mortem: what did I over-trust — public records, analogy, my own thesis? That paragraph is the most valuable text this document will ever contain.
